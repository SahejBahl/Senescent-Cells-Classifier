Introducing seneSENSE

The centralized platform for healthcare providers to identify senescence and promote human longevity.

What are Senescent Cells?

Senescent cells are human cells that have stopped dividing, but have not died. They can be harmful in that they may cause cancer and other skin-related diseases, if generated in excess.

How does senescent cell detection work?

Senescent cell detection is done by analyzing a sample of skin cells under a microscope, and injecting it with a fluorescent substance that highlights which cells are senescent by turning them blue. The problem lies in the fact that visually monitoring the occurrence of senescent cells does not aid in identifying whether a person is at risk for cancer.

Our solution involves a Machine Learning model, which takes the photos of senescent cells, procured from lab technicians, and presents a senescence confidence level. If the confidence level is above 80%, the patient is recommended to see their family doctor or primary physician to determine if they are at risk.
